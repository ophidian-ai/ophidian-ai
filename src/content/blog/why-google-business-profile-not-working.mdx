## Your Best Free Marketing Tool (That You're Probably Ignoring)

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is the first thing most people see when they search for your business. It shows up in Google Maps, in local search results, and in that prominent info box on the right side of the screen. It is free, powerful, and most small businesses are barely using it.

If your profile is not generating phone calls, website visits, or foot traffic, you are leaving money on the table. Here are the most common mistakes and exactly how to fix them.

## Mistake 1: Incomplete Profile Information

Google rewards complete profiles with better visibility. An incomplete profile is like a store with half the lights off — people assume you are closed or do not care.

**How to fix it:**

- Fill out every single field: business name, address, phone, website, hours, category, description, attributes
- Add your primary and secondary business categories (most businesses qualify for 3-5)
- Write a complete business description using words your customers actually search for
- Add service areas if you travel to customers
- List every service you offer with descriptions and pricing

This alone can improve your visibility significantly. Google's own data shows that complete profiles get 7x more clicks than empty ones.

## Mistake 2: No Photos (or Bad Photos)

Businesses with photos get 42% more direction requests and 35% more website clicks than those without. Yet most small business profiles have zero photos or just a blurry logo from 2019.

**How to fix it:**

- Upload at least 10 high-quality photos: storefront, interior, team, products/services in action
- Add new photos every month — Google favors active profiles
- Make sure your cover photo clearly shows what your business does
- Include photos of completed work if you are a service business
- Let customers tag photos too — it adds authenticity

You do not need a professional photographer. A modern phone camera in good lighting is more than enough. The key is showing real photos of your actual business, not stock imagery.

## Mistake 3: Ignoring Reviews

Reviews are the single biggest factor in local search ranking after your basic profile information. A business with 50 reviews and a 4.5 rating will consistently outrank a competitor with 3 reviews, even if the competitor has a better website.

**How to fix it:**

- Ask every satisfied customer for a review — in person, by email, or by text
- Make it easy: send them a direct link to your review page (search "Google review link generator" for the shortcut)
- Respond to every review, positive and negative, within 48 hours
- For negative reviews: acknowledge the issue, offer to make it right, and take the conversation offline
- Never buy fake reviews — Google catches them and will penalize your profile

A simple post-service text that says "Thanks for choosing us! If you had a good experience, a Google review would mean a lot" works better than any complicated review strategy.

## Mistake 4: Wrong or Outdated Hours

Nothing frustrates a potential customer more than driving to a business that is supposed to be open and finding it closed. Google tracks this, and so do your customers.

**How to fix it:**

- Double-check your regular hours right now
- Add special hours for every holiday and seasonal change
- Update hours immediately when they change — do not wait
- If you have different hours for different services, add those too
- Set holiday hours at the beginning of each year for all major holidays

Google also uses your hours to decide when to show your business in search results. If your hours say you close at 5 PM, someone searching at 5:30 PM may not see you at all.

## Mistake 5: Never Posting Updates

Google Business Profiles have a Posts feature that works like a mini social media feed. Most businesses never use it. That is a missed opportunity for visibility and engagement.

**How to fix it:**

- Post at least once per week: promotions, new services, tips, events, behind-the-scenes content
- Include a photo or image with every post
- Add a call-to-action button (Call now, Learn more, Book online)
- Posts expire after 7 days, so consistency matters more than perfection
- Share seasonal promotions, new products, and community involvement

Google Posts show up directly in your profile and signal to Google that your business is active and engaged. It is free advertising in the most visible spot on the internet.

## Mistake 6: Not Using the Q&A Section

Your GBP has a Questions & Answers section that anyone can contribute to. If you are not monitoring it, random people might be answering questions about your business — sometimes incorrectly.

**How to fix it:**

- Check your Q&A section weekly
- Seed it with your most common questions and answer them yourself
- Address any incorrect answers by posting the correct information
- Answer new questions within 24 hours

This section shows up prominently in your profile. Having 10-15 well-answered questions makes your business look responsive and professional.

## The Quick Win Checklist

Here is what you can do in the next 30 minutes to improve your profile:

1. Log into [Google Business Profile Manager](https://business.google.com)
2. Fill in any empty fields in your profile
3. Upload 5 new photos
4. Respond to any unanswered reviews
5. Write one Google Post about what you are working on this week
6. Check your hours and update if needed
7. Review and answer any pending Q&A items

These steps are free and can make a measurable difference in your local visibility within a few weeks.
