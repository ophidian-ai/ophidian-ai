## The Short Answer

A small business website in 2026 costs anywhere from $0 to $10,000+ depending on how you build it. That is a wide range, so let us break it down into the three main paths most small businesses take.

## Option 1: DIY Website Builders ($0 - $300/year)

Platforms like Wix, Squarespace, and WordPress.com let you drag and drop your way to a live site. Monthly costs run $16 to $45 depending on the plan, and you can often get started for free.

**The upside:** Low cost, fast setup, no technical skills required.

**The catch:** You get what you get. Templates look like every other site using the same template. Performance tends to be mediocre — most DIY sites score 40-60 on Google's speed test. SEO features are limited. And when something breaks or you need a custom feature, you are stuck.

For a business card site that just needs to exist, this can work. For anything that needs to generate leads, rank in Google, or represent a premium brand, the limitations add up fast.

## Option 2: Freelance Developer ($1,500 - $5,000)

Hiring a freelancer gets you a custom-designed site built to your specifications. Typical projects run 2-4 weeks and include 5-10 pages, mobile responsiveness, basic SEO setup, and a contact form.

**The upside:** You get something unique. A good freelancer will design around your brand, optimize for speed, and set up proper SEO foundations.

**The catch:** Quality varies enormously. Finding a reliable freelancer takes effort, timelines can slip, and ongoing support is hit or miss. Many freelancers build on WordPress, which means you inherit the maintenance burden — plugin updates, security patches, hosting management.

This is the most common path for small businesses that want something better than a template but are not ready for a full agency engagement.

## Option 3: Custom Agency Build ($2,200 - $6,000+)

A professional agency builds a fully custom site tailored to your business goals. This typically includes brand-aligned design, high-performance code, SEO optimization, analytics setup, and ongoing support.

**The upside:** Everything is built with purpose. The site loads fast, ranks well, converts visitors, and reflects your brand. You get a partner who handles the technical details so you can focus on running your business.

**The catch:** Higher upfront cost. But when you factor in the time you save, the leads you gain, and the professional impression you make, the ROI usually justifies the investment.

## What About Ongoing Costs?

Regardless of which path you choose, plan for these recurring expenses:

- **Domain name:** $10 - $15/year
- **Hosting:** $0 (Vercel free tier) to $20/month (traditional hosting)
- **SSL certificate:** Free with most modern platforms
- **Maintenance:** $100 - $200/month if you want someone else handling updates, security, and content changes
- **Email:** $6/user/month for Google Workspace or Microsoft 365

For our clients, hosting is included at no extra cost through Vercel's free tier, and optional maintenance starts at **$100/month** — covering security monitoring, uptime checks, and minor content updates.

## How to Decide What Is Right for You

Ask yourself three questions:

1. **Is your website your primary way of getting new customers?** If yes, invest in a custom build. A $3,000 website that generates 5 new customers per month at $500 each pays for itself in the first week.
2. **Do you have the time to build and maintain it yourself?** Most business owners underestimate how much time DIY takes. If your hourly rate is $75+, spending 40 hours on a Wix site costs you $3,000 in opportunity cost — the same as hiring a professional.
3. **How important is your first impression?** 75% of consumers judge a business's credibility by its website. A slow, templated site sends a message — and it is not the one you want.
