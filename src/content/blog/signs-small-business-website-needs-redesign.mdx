## Your Website Is Your Hardest-Working Employee

It works 24/7, talks to every potential customer, and shapes their first impression of your business. But unlike an employee you can observe and coach, a website can quietly underperform for months — or years — without you realizing it.

Here are five clear signs your site is due for a redesign, and what you can do about each one.

## Sign 1: It Takes More Than 3 Seconds to Load

Speed is not just a technical detail. It directly affects your bottom line. Google's own research shows that 53% of mobile visitors leave a site that takes longer than 3 seconds to load. Every second of delay reduces conversions by 7%.

**How to check:** Go to [Google PageSpeed Insights](https://pagespeed.web.dev), enter your URL, and look at your mobile score. Anything below 70 is a problem. Below 50 is an emergency.

**Common causes of slow sites:**

- Unoptimized images (the #1 culprit for most small business sites)
- Cheap shared hosting that slows down during peak hours
- Too many plugins or third-party scripts
- Outdated code or bloated page builders
- No caching or CDN configuration

**What a redesign fixes:** A modern site built with performance in mind loads in under 2 seconds. All of our client sites at OphidianAI score 90+ on Lighthouse — because performance is built into the code from day one, not bolted on after.

## Sign 2: It Is Not Mobile-Friendly

Over 60% of web traffic comes from mobile devices. For local businesses, that number is even higher — people searching "plumber near me" or "best restaurant in Columbus" are almost always on their phone.

If your site does not look and work perfectly on a phone screen, you are turning away the majority of your visitors.

**How to check:** Pull up your website on your phone. Try to navigate, read the text, and tap the buttons. If you have to pinch-zoom, scroll sideways, or squint to read anything, you have a problem.

**Red flags to watch for:**

- Text that is too small to read without zooming
- Buttons that are too close together to tap accurately
- Menus that do not collapse into a mobile-friendly hamburger menu
- Images that overflow the screen or load at desktop sizes
- Contact forms that are painful to fill out on a phone

**What a redesign fixes:** Modern responsive design adapts to every screen size automatically. Navigation becomes intuitive, text becomes readable, and buttons become tappable — all without maintaining a separate "mobile version" of your site.

## Sign 3: There Is No Clear Call to Action

Every page on your website should answer one question for the visitor: **"What do I do next?"** If the answer is not obvious within 5 seconds of landing on any page, you are losing potential customers.

**Common CTA problems:**

- No contact button or phone number visible on every page
- A "Contact Us" page buried three clicks deep in the navigation
- Generic CTAs like "Learn More" that do not tell visitors what happens next
- Multiple competing CTAs that create decision paralysis
- No CTA at all — just information with no next step

**What good CTAs look like:**

- "Book a Free Estimate" — specific, valuable, low-commitment
- "Call Us Now: (555) 123-4567" — prominent, clickable on mobile
- "See Our Work" — shows proof before asking for commitment
- "Get Your Free Website Audit" — trades value for contact info

**What a redesign fixes:** Strategic CTA placement throughout every page, designed to guide visitors naturally from curiosity to action. The goal is not to be pushy — it is to make the next step obvious and easy.

## Sign 4: The Design Looks Dated

Web design trends evolve quickly. A site that looked modern in 2021 can look noticeably outdated today — and visitors notice. A dated design does not just look bad; it signals that the business behind it might be outdated too.

**Signs your design has aged:**

- Small text on a white background with no visual hierarchy
- Stock photos that look generic and staged
- A cluttered layout trying to show everything on one page
- A design that has not been touched since the site launched
- A homepage slider or carousel (a pattern that has fallen out of favor for good reason — most visitors never see past the first slide)

**What modern design looks like:**

- Clean layouts with generous whitespace
- Clear visual hierarchy that guides the eye
- Authentic photography and purposeful illustrations
- Dark mode support and accessible contrast ratios
- Smooth animations and transitions that feel intentional
- Fast loading with optimized assets

**What a redesign fixes:** A fresh design built on current best practices instantly elevates your brand perception. The goal is not to be trendy — it is to look professional, trustworthy, and current.

## Sign 5: You Have No Idea How It Is Performing

If you cannot answer these questions, you are flying blind:

- How many visitors does your site get per month?
- Which pages do people visit most?
- Where are your visitors coming from?
- What percentage of visitors contact you or make a purchase?
- How does your site rank for your key search terms?

**Why this matters:** Without data, every decision about your business's online presence is a guess. You might be spending money on advertising that drives traffic to a page nobody converts on. You might have a blog post ranking on Google that you could double down on. You might be losing visitors on a specific page and never know it.

**What a redesign includes:** Proper analytics setup (Google Analytics, Search Console, and performance monitoring) so you can see exactly what is working and what is not. Every OphidianAI site includes analytics configuration and a monthly performance report for clients on a maintenance plan.
